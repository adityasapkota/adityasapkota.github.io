About me

I am Chetraj Pandey, a PhD student in Computer Science at Georgia State University. I am originally from Tikapur, a small town in far-western region of Nepal.

Currently, my research is centered at the crossroads of computer science and heliophysics, where I undertake tasks in developing and applying machine learning and deep learning techniques to solar physics problems. My work primarily involves the collection, curation, analysis and utilization of various data products, which includes sensor-based data, image data, and time-series data of solar physics domain. By leveraging these resources, I develop data-driven solutions to the problems in space weather forecasting (solar flares). These solutions transcends disciplinary boundaries and can be applicable in numerous real-world domains as well. Working in interdisciplinary research not only deepens my understanding of diverse problem domains but also equips me with the tools to craft effective data-driven solutions. I am committed to contributing to the advancement of both computer science and related interdisciplinary fields through my research.
